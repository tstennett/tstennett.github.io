Education in Africa

Introduction

Sub-Saharan Africa has the worst literacy rate in the world. Only 1 in 3 adults can read, which shows how poor the African education system is, and how terrible the situation is for people living in Africa.

Causes and Effects

Firstly, African education has been proven to be inadequate when it comes to mathematics, an important factor in determining whether the youth will enter middle class. The Southern and Eastern African Consortium for Monitoring Education Quality (SACMEQ) found that more than the majority of math teachers for grades one through six had failed to pass Math tests for their grade level. This displays how unqualified the teachers of South Africa are.

Secondly, Africa has the highest rate of teacher absenteeism. This shows the unprofessionalism of the African teachers, and how unserious the education system is treated.

Finally, Africa's education department had closed teacher training institutions in 1994. This deteriorates the quality of education in Africa, as countries such as the United States, who have leading standards in education, hold high standards for their teachers by utilizing said institutes.

Overall, this indicates the poor quality of education within Africa, and particularly Sub-Saharan Africa. This limits the opportunities and lives of the African people, and is a major obstacle in the growth of the African economy.
